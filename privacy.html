Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 18, 2026

The short version: Sweep runs entirely on your iPhone and your photos never leave your device. It uses no third-party trackers or analytics SDKs. The only data stored off-device is the private identifier from Sign in with Apple plus your Pro purchase status, kept in your iCloud (Apple CloudKit) so your unlock follows you across devices.

1. Our approach to privacy

Sweep ("the app", "we", "us") is a photo-cleaning utility for iPhone. We designed it on a simple principle: a tool that touches your entire photo library should never be able to see, copy, or upload it. Everything Sweep does happens locally on your device.

2. Data we collect

Almost nothing — and never your photos. The only data Sweep stores off your device is:

  • The opaque identifier from Sign in with Apple — an app-specific ID, never your real Apple ID, email, or phone number.
  • Your Pro purchase status (a simple paid/free flag and the App Store transaction id).

These are stored in Apple's CloudKit so your Pro unlock follows you across your devices. That is the entire backend — there is no other server. Sweep specifically does not collect:

  • Your photos, videos, or any content from your library.
  • Your name, email address, phone number, or contacts.
  • Location data, device identifiers for tracking, or advertising IDs.
  • Usage analytics, behavioral data, or crash telemetry of any kind.

3. How Sweep uses photo access

To do its job, Sweep asks for permission to access your Photos through Apple's standard iOS permission prompt. This access is used only on your device and only to:

  • Display your photos and videos so you can swipe through them.
  • Analyze photos locally (for Pro's smart finders such as duplicates, similar shots, blurry photos, and large videos).
  • Move photos you choose to bin into the iOS Recently Deleted album when you tap Clean.

Photos analyzed by Sweep are never copied off your device. You can review or revoke photo access at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security → Photos on your iPhone.

4. Deleting photos is recoverable

Sweep never permanently deletes anything on its own. When you tap Clean, the photos you binned are moved to your device's standard Recently Deleted album, where iOS keeps them for 30 days before removing them. During that window you can recover any photo directly in the Apple Photos app.

5. Sign in with Apple & iCloud sync

Sweep uses Sign in with Apple to identify you with an opaque, app-specific identifier — we never receive your real Apple ID, email, or phone number. That identifier and your Pro purchase status are stored in Apple's CloudKit so your unlock follows you across your devices. If you choose not to use iCloud, Sweep still works fully and your Pro unlock is restored through the App Store. No photo data is ever written to iCloud or anywhere off your device.

6. No third parties

Sweep includes no third-party advertising networks, no analytics or attribution SDKs, and no social-media trackers. The only backend is Apple's own infrastructure (StoreKit and CloudKit). We do not sell or share your data with any third party for advertising or profiling.

7. Purchases

Sweep Pro is a one-time in-app purchase. Payments are processed entirely by Apple through StoreKit and the App Store. We never see or store your payment details, card numbers, or Apple Account information. Apple may process purchase data under Apple's Privacy Policy. When you purchase, Sweep records a simple paid flag against your Sign in with Apple identifier in CloudKit so the unlock follows you across your devices.

8. Network use

The app never sends your photos or any library content over the network. The only network activity is Apple's App Store purchase/restore flow and CloudKit sync of your Pro status — both handled by Apple's systems, not a server of ours.

9. Children's privacy

Sweep does not collect personal information from anyone, including children. The app is a general-purpose utility and is not directed at children under the age of 13.

10. Changes to this policy

If we ever change how Sweep handles data, we will update this page and revise the "Last updated" date above. Material changes will be reflected here before they take effect.
